🚀 Indian Startup Funding Intelligence Report (2015–2020)
With Deep Dive: Quick Commerce Sector Analysis
📌 Project Overview
A comprehensive data analysis of 3,044 Indian startup funding rounds from 2015 to 2020 — uncovering where Indian VC money flows, which cities dominate, and how Quick Commerce giants like Swiggy, BigBasket and Grofers grew from seed to unicorn.

❓ Business Problem
Indian founders, analysts and job seekers lack a clear picture of:

Which sectors attract the most VC funding in India?
Which cities produce the most funded startups?
How did Quick Commerce funding evolve year by year?
Which investors are most active in the Indian ecosystem?
💡 Key Insights Discovered
🏙️ Bangalore raised ₹1,84,600 Crores — 4x more than Mumbai
📈 2017 was India's peak funding year — ₹86,000+ Crores raised
🛒 BigBasket led Quick Commerce — grew from $50M (2015) to $300M (2018) — 6x in 3 years
🦅 Ratan Tata was India's most active angel investor — 25 deals!
📉 COVID wiped 99% of deals — 993 deals (2016) → 7 deals (2020)
🏭 Consumer Internet dominated — 941 deals worth $6.25 Billion
💰 Grofers raised $120M in 2015 backed by SoftBank, Tiger Global, Sequoia
